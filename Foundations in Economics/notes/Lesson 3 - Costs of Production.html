The amount that a firm receives for the sale of its output is called its total rvenue. The amount that the firm pays to buy inputs is called its total cost. The total revenue minus total cost is a firm's profit.

Excplicit costs are costs that require an outlat of money by the firm (wages, equipment, etc.). Implicit costs are costs that do not require an outlay of money by the firm (using another skill for wages, income lost from reinvesting capital into a business, etc.).
Economic profit is the total revenue minus the total cost, including both implicit and explicit costs. Accounting profit is the total revenue minus the explicit cost.
The relationship between quantity of inputs used to make a good and the quantity of output of that good is the production function


The marginal product is the increase in output that arises from an additional unit of input.

A diminishing marginal product is the property whereby the marginal product of an input declines as the quantity of the input increases.
A fixed cost is a cost that does not vary with the quantity of output produced.

Variable cost is a cost that varies with the quantity of output produced.

Average variable cost is the variable cost divided by the quantity of output.

Average total cost is the total cost divided by the quantity of output.

Marginal cost is the increase in total cost that arises from an extra unity of production.







The efficient scale is the quantityt of output that minimizes average total cost.

Whenever marginal cost is less than average total cost, average total cost is falling. Whenever marginal cost is greater than average total cost, avergae total cost is rising.


  • Marginal cost eventually rises with the quantity of output.
  • The average-total-cost curve is U-shaped.
  • The marginal-cost curve crosses the average-total-cost curve at the minimum of average total cost.
Economies of scale is the property whereby long-run average total cost falls as the quantity of output increases.

Diseconomies of scale is the property whereby long-run average total cost rises as the quantity of output increases.

Constant returns of scale is the property whereby long-run average total costs stays the same as the quantity of output changes.
