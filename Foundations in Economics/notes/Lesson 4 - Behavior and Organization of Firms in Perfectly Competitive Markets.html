If a firm can influence the market price a the good it sells, it is said to have market power.

A competitive market is a market with many buyers and sellers trading identical products so that each buyer and seller is a price taker.

Buyers and sellers in competitive markets must accept the price the market determines and, therefore, are said to be price takers.

Average Revenue = TR / Q => for all firms, average revenue equals the price of the good.

Marginal Revenue the change in total revenue from an additional unit sold.

Average Revenue = TR / Q

Marginal Revenue = △TR / △Q

The profit mazimizing quantity can be found by comparing the marginal revenue to the marginal cost for each unit produced.




When marginal cost is less than marginal revenue, an increase in the quantity of production by 1 unit yields a greater profit. When marginal cost is greater than marginal revenue, a decrease in the quantity of production by 1 yeilds a greater profit. The profit maximizing level of output is reached when marginal revenue and marginal cost are exactly equal.
Because a firm's marginal-cost curve determines the quantity of the good the firm is willing to upply at any price, the marginal-cost curve is also the competitive firm's supply curve.

A shutdown refers to a short-run decision not to produce anything during a specific period of time because of current market conditions.

An exit refers to a long-run decision to leave the market.

A firm that shuts down still has to pay its fixed costs whereas a firm that exits does not have to pay any costs at all, fixed or variable. So a firm shuts down if the revenue that it would earn from producing is less than its variable costs of production.

Shutdown if TR < VC => TR/Q < VC/Q => P < AVC

A sunk cost is a cost that has already been committed and cannot be recovered.

Exit if TR < TC => TR/Q < TC/Q => P < ATC


Enter if P > ATC
The competitive firm's long-run supply curve is the portion of its marginal-cost curve that lies above the average total cost.






A marginal firm is the firm that would exit the market if the price were any lower.

Because firms can enter and exit more easily in the long run than in the short run the long-run supply curve is typically more elastic that the short-run supply curve.
